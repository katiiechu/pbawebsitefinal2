The Rift

This project explores the friction between the world's two largest economies when it comes to climate policy. Here, climate experts tackle the reasons for China's dominance in green energy and emissions reductions (not without addressing its challenges) and predict its future direction. We also consider that the US lags behind China in this capacity, examining why that is the case.


Dr. Kyle Chan is a foreign policy fellow at the John L. Thornton China Center of the Brookings Institution. His interests lie in China's industrial policy, where leveraged technologies like semiconductors, electric vehicles, and batteries are used to bolster their economy.


Dr. Barry Rabe is the Arthur Thurnau Professor Emeritus of Environmental Policy at the University of Michigan. His work focuses on the durability and efficacy of U.S. climate policy.


Li Shuo is the Director of China Climate Hub at the Asia Society Policy Institute and the Global Policy Advisor for Greenpeace East Asia. He is dedicated to analyzing the international sphere's relationship with Chinese climate agendas.
